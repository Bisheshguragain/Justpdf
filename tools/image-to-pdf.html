JPG vs PNG: Which Format to Use?

JPG/JPEG is ideal for photographs and images with many colors. It uses compression to create smaller file sizes, making it perfect for photos from cameras and phones. PNG is better for images with text, logos, or transparent backgrounds. It maintains perfect quality but creates larger files. Both formats convert to PDF perfectly - choose based on your original image type.

Tips for Best Results

  • Use consistent image sizes and orientations for a professional-looking PDF
  • Arrange images in logical order before converting (you can rearrange in the preview)
  • For scanned documents, ensure images are clear and properly cropped
  • Use high-resolution images (300 DPI or higher) for print-quality PDFs
  • Compress large images before conversion if file size is a concern
  • Name your images sequentially (photo1, photo2, etc.) for automatic ordering
  • Remove unwanted images from the selection before converting

How many images can I convert at once?

There's no strict limit! You can upload and convert dozens or even hundreds of images in a single PDF. Very large batches may take a bit longer to process.

Will the image quality be reduced in the PDF?

No! Our converter preserves the original image quality and resolution. Your photos will look exactly as they did in the original files.

Can I change the order of images before converting?

Yes! You can see a preview of all selected images and they'll be converted in the order you selected them. Upload images in your desired sequence.

What's the maximum file size I can convert?

There's no imposed file size limit, but very large images (100+ MB each) may take longer to process depending on your device's performance.

Can I convert HEIC or RAW image formats?

Currently we support JPG, JPEG, and PNG formats. For HEIC (iPhone photos) or RAW files, please convert them to JPG first using your phone or photo software.

Will the PDF maintain the original image dimensions?

Yes! Each image becomes a page in the PDF with the same dimensions as the original. Vertical images create portrait pages, horizontal images create landscape pages.

Can I add images from my phone camera directly?

Yes! On mobile devices, you can select photos directly from your camera roll or take new photos to convert to PDF.

Is there a way to compress the PDF after conversion?

Yes! After converting, you can use our separate "Compress PDF" tool to reduce the file size while maintaining good quality.
