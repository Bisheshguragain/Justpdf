Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add page numbers to a PDF?

Upload your PDF using the upload button, choose your preferred position (e.g., bottom-center), select a number format (e.g., "Page 1 of 10"), customize font size if needed, and click "Add Page Numbers". Your numbered PDF will be ready to download in seconds.

Can I add page numbers to PDF for free?

Yes! JustPdf's page numbering tool is completely free with no watermarks, no registration required, and no file limits. Add page numbers to as many PDFs as you need, absolutely free. All processing happens in your browser, so your files remain 100% private.

Is it safe to add page numbers to PDFs online?

Yes, absolutely. All processing happens client-side in your browser using JavaScript. Your PDF files never leave your device and are not uploaded to any server. We cannot see, access, or store your documents in any way. 100% private and secure.

Will adding page numbers change my PDF content?

No, page numbers are added as a new layer on top of your existing content. All your original text, images, formatting, and layout remain exactly as they were. Only the page numbers are added in your chosen position.

Can I remove page numbers later?

Once page numbers are added to a PDF, they become part of the document. If you need to remove them, you would need to use a PDF editor. We recommend keeping your original PDF file before adding numbers, in case you need the unnumbered version later.

What's the file size limit?

While there's no strict limit, we recommend PDFs under 50MB for best performance. Very large documents may take longer to process or could fail on devices with limited memory. Most standard documents (10-200 pages) process instantly.

Can I start numbering from a specific page?

Yes! Use the "Start numbering from" option to begin with any number. For example, if you want the first page to show "Page 5", set start number to 5. This is useful for multi-part documents or when you want to exclude cover pages from numbering.

Which position is most professional?

Bottom-center is the most common for business documents and reports. Academic papers often use top-right. Books typically alternate outer corners (left for even pages, right for odd pages). Choose the position that matches your document type or industry standards.
