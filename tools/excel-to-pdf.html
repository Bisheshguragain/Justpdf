XLSX vs XLS: What's the Difference?

XLSX (Excel 2007 and later) is the modern XML-based format with better compression, corruption recovery, and support for larger spreadsheets. XLS is the legacy binary format used in Excel 2003 and earlier. Our converter supports both formats, though XLSX is recommended for optimal quality and file size.

Tips for Best Results

  • Ensure your Excel file has proper page breaks if you want specific page divisions in the PDF
  • Use Excel's "Page Layout" view to preview how your spreadsheet will appear in PDF format
  • Adjust print area and page setup in Excel before conversion for optimal PDF output
  • Remove hidden rows or columns you don't want in the final PDF
  • Consider setting print titles for tables that span multiple pages
  • Use landscape orientation for wide spreadsheets to avoid content being cut off

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I convert password-protected Excel files?

You'll need to remove the password protection in Excel before converting. Open the file in Excel, remove the password, save, and then convert to PDF.

Will formulas be calculated in the PDF?

Yes! The PDF will show the calculated results of all formulas as they appear in your Excel file. However, the formulas themselves won't be editable in the PDF.

How many sheets can I convert at once?

You can convert Excel files with multiple worksheets. Each sheet will become a separate page in the resulting PDF document.

What's the maximum file size I can convert?

There's no strict file size limit, but very large files (100+ MB) may take longer to process depending on your device's capabilities.

Can I convert Excel files created in Google Sheets?

Yes! Download your Google Sheets file as XLSX format (File > Download > Microsoft Excel), then convert it using our tool.

Will charts and graphs be included in the PDF?

Yes, embedded charts and graphs will be rendered in the PDF. The quality depends on how they're displayed in Excel's print preview.
