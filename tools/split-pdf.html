How to Split a PDF Online

Splitting PDF files has never been easier with JustPdf's free online PDF splitter. Whether you need to extract specific pages from a large document, divide a PDF into smaller chunks, or separate each page into individual files, our tool provides flexible options to meet your needs. The entire process happens in your browser, ensuring your sensitive documents remain private and secure.

Step-by-Step Guide to Splitting PDFs

  1. Upload Your PDF: Click the upload area or drag and drop your PDF file. Supported formats include standard PDFs, scanned documents, and encrypted PDFs (if you have permission).
  2. Choose Split Mode: Select from three splitting options:
    • Extract Page Range: Extract specific pages by entering ranges like "1-5, 8, 10-12"
    • Extract Each Page Separately: Create individual PDF files for each page
    • Split into Equal Chunks: Divide your PDF into equally-sized sections
  3. Specify Pages or Chunk Size: Depending on your selected mode, enter the page ranges or chunk size (pages per file).
  4. Split PDF: Click the "Split PDF" button and wait for processing to complete.
  5. Download Split Files: Download your extracted PDF files individually or all at once.

Why Split PDF Files?

There are many practical reasons to split PDF documents:

  • Share Specific Sections: Extract and share only relevant pages instead of the entire document
  • Reduce File Size: Create smaller files that are easier to email or upload
  • Organize Documents: Separate chapters, sections, or topics into individual files
  • Extract Important Pages: Pull out key pages like contracts, invoices, or reports
  • Archive Efficiently: Divide large archives into manageable sections
  • Prepare for Printing: Split documents to print specific sections
  • Digital Workflows: Create separate files for different departments or teams

Understanding Split Modes

Each split mode serves different purposes:

Extract Page Range

Best for: Extracting specific sections, chapters, or non-consecutive pages. Example: Extract pages 1-5, 15, and 20-25 from a 100-page document.

Extract Each Page Separately

Best for: Creating individual files for each page, sharing single pages, or organizing by page. Results in N files for an N-page document.

Split into Equal Chunks

Best for: Dividing large documents into manageable sections of equal size. Example: Split a 60-page document into 10-page chunks (6 files).

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I split a PDF file online?

Upload your PDF file, choose your split mode (extract pages, split into chunks, or extract each page separately), specify the pages or chunk size, and click Split PDF. Download your split files instantly.

Is it safe to split PDFs online?

Yes, our PDF splitter processes files entirely in your browser using client-side JavaScript. Your files never leave your device, ensuring complete privacy and security.

Can I extract specific pages from a PDF?

Yes, you can extract specific pages by entering page ranges (e.g., 1-5, 8, 10-12) or extract each page as a separate PDF file.

What split modes are available?

Three split modes are available: Extract page range (specific pages), Extract each page separately (creates individual PDFs), and Split into equal chunks (divide by pages per chunk).

Is there a file size limit for splitting PDFs?

While there's no strict limit, very large files (over 100MB) may take longer to process depending on your device's performance. For best results, use moderately-sized PDFs.

Can I split password-protected PDFs?

You can split password-protected PDFs if you have permission to modify them. Use our Unlock PDF tool first to remove password protection, then split the unlocked file.

Does splitting reduce PDF quality?

No, splitting is a lossless operation. The extracted pages maintain the same quality, resolution, and formatting as the original PDF.

Can I split PDFs on mobile devices?

Yes, our tool is fully responsive and works on smartphones and tablets. However, larger files may process slower on mobile devices with limited resources.
